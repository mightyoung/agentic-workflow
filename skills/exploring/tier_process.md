# EXPLORING - Core Process

## Entry Criteria

Exploring phase begins when:
1. User explicitly wants deep discussion: "I want to explore this idea deeply"
2. User's thinking is fuzzy/incomplete/contradictory: vague expressions, inconsistencies, uncertainty
3. Task involves discovery: "Why is this true?", "What's really going on?", creative scenarios
4. Keywords: "experiment", "analyze", "I have an idea", "figure out", "deep", "essence", "root cause", uncertainty signals

## Distinction from OFFICE-HOURS

| Dimension | OFFICE-HOURS | EXPLORING |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Goal | Clarify requirement structure | Uncover deep thinking |
| Method | Structured Q&A | Open-ended Socratic |
| Depth | Surface clarification | Deep excavation |
| Rounds | 2-3 rounds | Continuous until saturated |

## Core Process Steps

**Round 1: Opening & Invitation**
Introduce Socratic experiment; invite user to explore deeply and truthfully. Ask opening question: "What do you want to explore?"

**Middle Rounds: Follow-Up Framework**
After each answer, choose follow-up type based on content:
- **Assumptions**: "You mentioned [idea]. The assumption here is [extracted assumption]. Is this assumption true? What if it were false?"
- **Boundaries**: "If [boundary condition changes], does the plan still work? E.g., 100x users, 1/10 time, different team?"
- **Contradictions**: "You said [view A], but earlier you mentioned [view B]. How do you reconcile this tension?"
- **Essence**: "Why specifically this? Not something else? Starting from zero, would you choose the same?"
- **Potential**: "Based on our discussion, I see a possible direction: [direction]. Have you thought about this? What if we combined [A] and [B]?"

**Final Round: Synthesis & Confirmation**
Summarize observed falsehoods, limitations, potential, unconscious constructs. Ask user: "Do these match your real thinking? What did I misunderstand?"

## Key Decision Points

- **When to switch question types**: If answer shows assumption, challenge it; if describes concrete plan, test boundaries; if contradictions emerge, probe them
- **Saturation detection**: 2-3 consecutive rounds with no new discoveries → approaching saturation
- **User resistance**: If user unwilling to go deeper → respect boundary; transition to DONE or BLOCKED

## Exit Criteria Details

All should be true:
- At least 1 falsehood identified and user confirms
- At least 1 limitation identified and user confirms
- At least 1 potential or possibility identified
- Connected to user's unconscious constructs (things they hadn't explicitly thought)
- User declares saturation or confirms final synthesis
